The environment
One of the largest Victorian State Government contracts. Multi-regulated, heavily audited and security-bound, the kind of highly secure environment a bank would recognise, with no appetite for risk.
The real barrier
Not the technology. People blocked AI outright, assuming it was insecure. The work was changing that mentality, proving AI could be safe, controlled and genuinely helpful here.
What I did
Recognised the opportunity, educated stakeholders, built a working prototype to demonstrate it rather than pitch it, put the controls on, and earned the approvals to integrate it into the organisation.
Where it landed
A robust, adopted capability running in a heavily audited, fully governed setting. Evidenced, signed off, and simple enough for senior executives to use unaided, running at a small fraction of the cost of the manual effort it absorbed.
◆ The harness that made it safe
The controls were the unlock, the reason a risk-averse, heavily audited organisation allowed it in at all.
  • Human review and sign-off at every decision gate
  • Source traceability grounded in a controlled data environment
  • Validation checkpoints and output verification
  • Grounded retrieval that cites its sources, or abstains honestly
  • Audit-ready evidence, decision logs and documentation
  • Confidentiality boundaries and escalation pathways

06AI Governance

Governance that lets AI move, safely.

For a bank, or any organisation a regulator can inspect, the job is not AI enthusiasm. It is making AI safe, explainable, auditable and regulator-ready, while still helping teams ship. I design and operate enterprise AI governance, I don't just write the policy and hand it over.

01

Enterprise Governance Frameworks

Establish enterprise-wide AI governance that enables safe, transparent and accountable adoption, translating responsible AI principles into practical controls, decision rights, assurance processes and delivery guardrails.

02

Risk, Controls and Assurance

Embed risk-based controls across the AI lifecycle: use case assessment, model risk classification, approval pathways, control testing, evidence capture, audit readiness and ongoing monitoring, with the rigour banks apply to cyber, privacy and financial risk.

03

Responsible and Ethical AI

Fairness, transparency, explainability, privacy, security, human oversight and customer protection, converted from principle into operational standards and measurable controls rather than statements of intent.

04

Delivery Enablement

Governance that helps teams move faster by making the safe path clear. Proportionate, embedded and scalable, partnering with AI, data, product, legal, privacy, risk and compliance instead of sitting as a late checkpoint.

05

Full Lifecycle Governance

From use case intake and risk triage, through data assessment, model validation and deployment approval, to post-implementation monitoring, incident response and periodic review.

06

Executive and Board Visibility

Give leaders a clear line of sight on what AI is in use, where the risk sits, which controls exist, what value is landing and what needs escalation, through plain reporting, governance forums, risk dashboards and escalation paths.

07

Third Party and Vendor AI Risk

Govern externally supplied and embedded AI across SaaS, cloud and analytics platforms: data usage, contractual controls, security and privacy obligations, and ongoing assurance that partners meet the same risk standard.

08

Cross Functional Influence

Align senior stakeholders across technology, data, risk, compliance, legal, privacy, cyber and internal audit around common standards and accountable decisions, without owning every delivery team.

09

Practical AI Literacy

Enough hands-on understanding of AI to challenge model behaviour, data quality, limitations and monitoring directly, not just review it from a distance.
